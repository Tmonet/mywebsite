Using the Concepts

Using The Concepts to Create: recommended materials(if done by hand) and instruction on how to use the concepts above.

  • Crescent Rendr No Show Thru Paper. It is the best for ink and markers. If you do not have access to this paper I recommend using any of the Strathmore inking papers.
  • Obviously a pencil, preferably not mechanical because the line work will come out sloppy.
  • A Prismacolor inking pen in .3 , .5 and a B or C pen(or anything similar to the Prismacolor brand).
  • Should have an eraser.
  • Copic markers. Layer the colors from lightest to darkest to avoid messy marker tips. The best brands are Prismacolor and Blick Art Materials copic. These markers blend really nicely.
  • White gel pen. This is optional, but it gives your art a nice finish.

Step by Step. This is a cartoon, so NOTHING is perfect. I am not a professional, and I am still learning.

First, draw out the SHAPE you want your character to have. I chose SQUARE with round edges so my character comes off as playful. Heads up, I am drawing my dog.

Next, sketch out the facial/body/clothing features. He is a Boston, so that would explain his design.

After you are HAPPY with your sketch, ink it. This is where the Prismacolor pens would come in handy. I mainly used .3 , but use the one you feel fits best.

Now for the fun part: coloring. I used the Copic brand. The markers are listed above in ORDER of use. I used a blue color scheme because as said before, blue means loyalty. My dog is very loyal, so, I thought the color fit. I used the lightest blue first, even though it is hard to see. Light colors first.
